Privacy policy

Last updated: January 28, 2026

01 Introduction

Dislikes ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy policy explains how our application accesses, uses, and protects your Google user data when you use our service.

02 Data We Access

Our application requests access to the following Google user data through the YouTube Data API:

  • Your YouTube rating history (specifically identifying videos you have disliked).
  • Basic video metadata (titles, channel names, thumbnails, view counts).

We use the https://www.googleapis.com/auth/youtube.readonly scope to ensure read-only access to your data.

03 How We Use Your Data

The data we access is used solely to provide the core functionality of the application: displaying, searching, and sorting your list of disliked videos. This processing happens entirely within your web browser.

04 Data Protection & Security

We do not store your personal YouTube data on any servers. All information is fetched directly from Google's APIs and resides only in your browser's temporary memory while the app is open.

To protect your sensitive data, we implement the following mechanisms:

  • Encryption in Transit: All communication between your browser and Google's servers is encrypted using industry-standard Transport Layer Security (TLS/HTTPS).
  • Local Processing: Data is processed entirely on your local device, ensuring it is never transmitted to or stored on our servers.
  • Token Security: We store your OAuth authentication token in your browser's localStorage. This token is used exclusively to authorize requests to Google on your behalf and is never shared.

05 Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, or trade your Google user data with any third parties. Your data never leaves the connection between your browser and Google's official endpoints.

06 User Control & Revocation

You can revoke the application's access to your data at any time by:

  • Clicking "Sign out" within the application.
  • Removing the application's access in your Google Account Security settings.

07 Data Retention & Deletion

We follow a strict data minimization and limited retention policy:

  • User Data: Since we do not store your YouTube data on our servers, there is no permanent record of your data in our systems. Data held in your browser's memory is deleted immediately when the application tab is closed.
  • Authentication Tokens: The OAuth token stored in your browser's localStorage is retained to keep you signed in. You can delete this token and all associated local state at any time by clicking the "Sign out" button within the application.
  • Account Deletion: To "delete" your account or data from this application, simply sign out and revoke permissions in your Google Security settings. Since we hold no server-side data, this effectively removes all footprints of your usage.
