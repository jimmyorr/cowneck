To solve for the y-intercept (b) of a parallel line passing through a specific point (x₁, y₁), you use the same slope (m) from your original equation.

b = y₁ - mx₁

Step-by-Step Derivation:

  1. Identify the Slope: Parallel lines have identical slopes. If your original line is y=mx+b, the slope of your new line is also m.
  2. Set up the Equation: Start with the slope-intercept form using the coordinates of your point (x₁, y₁): y₁ = m(x₁) + b
  3. Isolate b: Subtract mx₁ from both sides: b = y₁ - mx₁

Gemini

Think of a line as having two main traits: Direction (Slope/m) and Position (Y-intercept/b).

  • The "Sliding Ruler" Concept: Imagine a ruler on graph paper. The slope (m) "locks" the ruler at a specific angle. The point (x,y) is a pin stuck in the paper. To find the new line, you slide the ruler up or down until its edge touches the pin. The point where the ruler crosses the Y-axis is your new b.
  • The Logic of b = y - mx: Mathematically, the equation y = mx + b is a statement of balance. When you rearrange it to b = y - mx, you are asking: "I am currently at height y. I know the slope m accounts for a certain amount of that height based on how far over (x) I am. If I subtract the height caused by the slope (mx), what is left over?" That "leftover" is b.

You

How does this algebra and Descartes relate to the enlightenment

Gemini

Coordinate Geometry was one of the primary sparks that ignited the Enlightenment.

  1. Rejection of Tradition: Descartes famously decided to doubt everything until he could prove it with mathematical certainty, arguing that Reason is universal.
  2. "Mathematization" of Nature: The Cartesian grid allowed scientists to map the physical world onto a mathematical grid, leading to the idea of the "Clockwork Universe."
  3. Democratization of Knowledge: By turning complex algebraic problems into visual lines, Descartes made high-level math more accessible and standardized.

What was euclidean geometry avout

Gemini

Euclidean Geometry is the study of shapes on a flat surface using pure logic, traditionally using only a straightedge and a compass. It was compiled by Euclid around 300 BC in The Elements. It focused on the Axiomatic Method—building complex proofs from simple, self-evident truths.

You

Teach me how to use proofs

Gemini

A proof is like a legal argument. It uses a Two-Column structure:

  • Statements (Left): What is true.
  • Reasons (Right): Why it is true (Definitions, Properties, Theorems).

Example: To prove x=5 in 2x+5=15, you list each algebraic step as a statement and the property used (e.g., Subtraction Property) as the reason.
